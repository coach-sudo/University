What is—and is not—verified

Journal records come from Crossref; “journal article” does not by itself prove peer review, so the journal’s policy should be checked before a source is treated as authoritative. Books come from Open Library, overviews from Wikipedia, tools from GitHub, and videos from YouTube. Citation counts, authorship, venue, recency, conflicts, and retractions require deeper verification before high-stakes use. Uploaded material is treated as the learner’s perspective, not automatically as fact.
